The Dataset
The dataset contains 6,409 cases with age, gender, height, weight, and body fat percentage, and 195 cases also include workout frequency, session duration, and workout type.
Dataset Distribution
The Model
A Random Forest Regressor was used to train scikit-learn models for predicting body fat percentage and workout metrics. The other body metrics in the report, such as BMI, comes from known equations. A Random Forest Regressor is an ensemble machine learning model that builds multiple decision trees and combines their predictions to improve accuracy and reduce overfitting. It is capable of handling complex datasets, capturing non-linear relationships, and work well with both categorical and numerical data. Additionally, it is robust to noise and missing data, making it a reliable choice for real-world datasets.

Body Fat Prediction
For body fat prediction, the model achieves an overall R² of 0.89, indicating high accuracy. For males, it explains 73% of the variance in body fat (R² = 0.73) with a Mean Absolute Error (MAE) of 2.25. For females, the R² improves to 0.85, and the MAE is lower at 1.82, demonstrating better performance for predicting body fat in women.
The predictions were most influenced by gender (weight = 0.46), followed by weight (0.34), age (0.11), and height (0.10), indicating that gender and weight are the strongest predictors of body fat percentage.

Other Metric Calculation
Referencing reliable research paper equations, the other body metrics are calculated and displayed on the report page:
  • Body Fat Weight = Body Fat Percentage × Weight
  • Fat Free Body Weight = (1 - Body Fat Percentage) × Weight
  • Body Mass Index (BMI) = Weight / Height² [metric]
  • Body Metabolic Rate (BMR) for Female = 447.593 + (9.247 × Weight) + (3.098 × Height) - (4.330 × Age)
  • Body Metabolic Rate (BMR) for Male = 88.362 + (13.397 × Weight) + (4.799 × Height) - (5.677 × Age)
  • Total Body Water = (Fat Free Body Weight × 0.73) / Weight
Workout Prediction
For workout metrics, the predictions were less accurate. Workout frequency and session duration had moderate R² scores of 0.34 and 0.52, indicating limited predictive reliability. Predicting workout type was particularly challenging, with low precision and recall across categories and an overall accuracy of 23%, reflecting the difficulty in distinguishing workout types using the available features.
For workout metrics, the importance of features varied across prediction tasks:
  • Workout Type: Weight (0.29) and body fat percentage (0.28) were the most significant predictors, followed by height (0.21) and age (0.21), with gender (0.01) having minimal impact.
  • Workout Frequency: Body fat percentage (0.58) was the dominant factor, significantly outweighing weight (0.17), height (0.12), and age (0.12), while gender (0.006) contributed very little.
  • Workout Session Duration: Body fat percentage (0.63) was again the strongest predictor, followed by weight (0.14), height (0.11), and age (0.11), with gender (0.008) having negligible influence.
